# Feature Candidates

## P0: Core Calendar Coverage

| Candidate | Why | Effort | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Expand official macro calendars | Macro/quant users need inflation, labor, growth, consumption, housing, trade, and PMI release dates, not GDP only. | Medium | Official pages vary by format. |
| Add S&P 500 and major-cap earnings universe | Current earnings coverage is too tied to latest movers. S&P 500 and blue-chip earnings are required for market background. | Medium | Future earnings dates are often estimates. |
| Add market session and structure events | Holidays, early closes, OPEX, quarterly expirations, and index rebalances explain liquidity/calendar clustering. | Medium | Some sources are PDFs/XLSX and update annually. |
| Add source authority and confirmation badges | Users must distinguish official macro/FOMC dates from estimated earnings. | Low to Medium | Requires consistent normalization. |

## P1: Broader Market Background

| Candidate | Why | Effort | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Treasury auctions and refunding | Rates-sensitive strategies need auction calendar context. | Medium | Schedule and result pages are separate. |
| Weekly claims, sentiment, confidence, EIA | These releases affect macro narratives and sector context. | Medium | Some sources may have limited structured access. |
| Dividends, splits, IPOs | Useful for stock workflows and corporate-action awareness. | Medium | Provider/licensing issues likely. |

## P2: Premium or Later Coverage

| Candidate | Why | Effort | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Investor days, conferences, capital markets days | Useful for single-stock research. | High | Reliable coverage is often paid. |
| Global central banks and global macro | Useful for macro portfolios. | High | Time zone, localization, and source breadth grow quickly. |
| M&A, regulatory events, litigation calendars | Useful for event-driven workflows. | High | Often unstructured and news-driven. |
